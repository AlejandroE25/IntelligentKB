VPN Setup with Cisco AnyConnect

VPN, Cisco AnyConnect, NetID, off-campus, network, remote access, tunnel
[Internal Staff Note] VPN gateway address: vpn.university.edu. For split-tunnel issues escalate to Network Services team. Verify the user is using the correct NetID format (without @university.edu).

Overview

The Virtual Private Network (VPN) allows you to securely connect to university network resources from off-campus using Cisco AnyConnect. This is required for accessing library databases, internal systems, and department file shares when you are not on the campus network.

Requirements

  • Active NetID and password
  • Duo two-factor authentication enrolled
  • Cisco AnyConnect Secure Mobility Client (version 4.10 or later)
  • Windows 10/11 or macOS 11 or later (Linux also supported)

Installing Cisco AnyConnect

  1. Visit the software download portal at software.university.edu and sign in with your NetID.
  2. Search for Cisco AnyConnect and download the installer for your operating system.
  3. Run the installer and accept the license agreement.
  4. Select only the VPN component during installation (other components are optional).
  5. Click Install and wait for the process to complete, then click Finish.

Connecting to the VPN

  1. Launch Cisco AnyConnect Secure Mobility Client.
  2. In the server address field, enter vpn.university.edu and click Connect.
  3. Enter your NetID (username only, without @university.edu) and NetID password.
  4. When prompted for a second factor, approve the Duo push notification on your registered device, or enter a Duo passcode.
  5. Once authenticated, the VPN icon in your taskbar/menu bar will show a lock, indicating you are connected.

Disconnecting from the VPN

  1. Click the Cisco AnyConnect icon in the taskbar or menu bar.
  2. Click Disconnect.

Troubleshooting

  • Connection times out: Temporarily disable any third-party firewall or antivirus and try again. If successful, add vpn.university.edu as a trusted host in your security software.
  • "Authentication failed" error: Confirm you are using your NetID (not your full email address) and that your password has not expired at netid.university.edu.
  • Duo push not received: Check your mobile device's internet connection. You can also use a Duo passcode from the Duo mobile app.
  • AnyConnect won't install: Ensure you have administrator privileges on your computer. On macOS, you may need to allow the installer in System Preferences → Security & Privacy.
  • VPN connects but resources are unreachable: Try disconnecting, waiting 30 seconds, and reconnecting. If the problem persists, contact the Help Desk.
