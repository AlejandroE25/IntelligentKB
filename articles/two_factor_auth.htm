Setting Up Duo Two-Factor Authentication

Duo, two-factor, 2FA, MFA, authentication, security, NetID, mobile, phone, token
[Internal Staff Note] Hardware token bypass codes available through the Identity Admin Portal. Temporary bypass codes expire after 24 hours or first use (whichever comes first). Do not issue bypass codes over email — phone verification required.

Overview

Duo Security provides two-factor authentication (2FA) for university systems. When signing in, you verify your identity with both your NetID password and a second factor (Duo push, passcode, or hardware token). This protects your account even if your password is compromised.

Supported Second Factors

  • Duo Push — A push notification to the Duo Mobile app (recommended)
  • Duo Passcode — A 6-digit code generated in the Duo Mobile app
  • SMS Passcode — A code sent via text message to your registered phone
  • Hardware Token — A physical token issued by the Help Desk (for users without smartphones)

Enrolling a Smartphone with Duo Mobile

  1. Navigate to netid.university.edu and sign in.
  2. Click Manage Two-Factor Authentication.
  3. Click Add a new device and select Mobile phone.
  4. Enter your phone number and select your device type (iOS or Android).
  5. Install the Duo Mobile app from the App Store or Google Play.
  6. On the enrollment page, click I have Duo Mobile installed, then use the app to scan the QR code displayed on screen.
  7. Click Continue to complete enrollment.

Adding a Backup Device

It is strongly recommended to register a backup device in case your primary device is unavailable.

  1. Sign in to netid.university.edu.
  2. Click Manage Two-Factor Authentication → Add a new device.
  3. Follow the enrollment steps for the additional device.

Using Duo When Logging In

  1. Enter your NetID and password on any protected university login page.
  2. The Duo prompt appears. Select your preferred second factor.
  3. For Duo Push: tap Approve on your mobile device when the notification arrives.
  4. For a Passcode: open the Duo Mobile app, copy the 6-digit code, and enter it in the prompt.

Troubleshooting

  • Push notification not received: Ensure your phone has an active internet connection. Check that notifications are enabled for Duo Mobile in your phone settings. You can also use a passcode from the app instead.
  • Duo Mobile shows wrong code: Verify your phone's date and time are set to automatic/network time.
  • Lost or new phone: Contact the Help Desk immediately to deactivate the old device and enroll a new one.
  • Locked out with no device available: Contact the Help Desk in person with a photo ID to obtain a temporary bypass code.
