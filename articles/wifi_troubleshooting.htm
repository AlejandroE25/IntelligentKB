Connecting to Campus Wi-Fi (Eduroam)

Wi-Fi, wireless, eduroam, campus network, internet, NetID, connection, network
[Internal Staff Note] Eduroam RADIUS server: radius.university.edu. For persistent auth failures, check the wireless controller dashboard (controller.network.university.edu). Guest Wi-Fi credentials are rotated weekly; current password available on the internal staff portal.

Overview

The university provides two main wireless networks:

  • eduroam — Encrypted, authenticated network for students and employees. Use this for all regular university activities. Credentials: netid@university.edu and your NetID password.
  • UGuest — Open guest network for visitors. No login required, but usage is limited and unencrypted. Not recommended for sensitive activities.

Connecting to Eduroam — Windows

  1. Click the Wi-Fi icon in the system tray.
  2. Select eduroam from the list of networks.
  3. When prompted, enter your full email-format NetID: netid@university.edu and your NetID password.
  4. If asked about the server certificate, click Connect to accept the university's certificate.

Connecting to Eduroam — macOS

  1. Click the Wi-Fi icon in the menu bar.
  2. Select eduroam.
  3. Enter your username as netid@university.edu and your NetID password.
  4. Click Join. You may be prompted to trust a certificate — click Trust.

Connecting to Eduroam — iOS and Android

  1. Open Settings → Wi-Fi.
  2. Select eduroam.
  3. Enter netid@university.edu as the username and your NetID password.
  4. Accept the certificate when prompted.

Troubleshooting

  • Authentication failed: Ensure you are using netid@university.edu (with the domain) and your current NetID password. If your password recently changed, forget the network and reconnect.
  • Limited or no connectivity: Forget the network, restart your device's Wi-Fi, and reconnect.
  • Can't see eduroam: Ensure your device's Wi-Fi adapter is enabled. Try moving closer to an access point. If in a basement or thick-walled area, signal may be weak.
  • Certificate errors: Make sure your device's date and time are correct. If errors persist, contact the Help Desk.
  • Keeps disconnecting: Check for IP address conflicts by renewing your DHCP lease (Windows: ipconfig /renew; macOS: toggle Wi-Fi off/on).
