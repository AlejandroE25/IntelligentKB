NetID Claim, Students, Troubleshooting Errors and solutions

Edit page
A student contacts the Help Desk about an error with the NetID Claim service.
Help Desk: For security reasons, you may never provide a customer with their activation code. They should retrieve it by using the appropriate method for their affiliation. 

First, before you proceed, please check for these common issues:

  • Make sure the student has accepted admission in the MyIllini Portal before trying to claim their NetID.

  • Make sure the student has allowed more than 12 hours between accepting admission and trying to claim.

  • Make sure the activation code has not expired if the student deferred acceptance for more than a year. See Identity Management, NetID Activation Code Incorrect or Expired

Next, check and see if they actually claimed their NetID:

This happens a lot more often than you'd think. The student says they finished claiming their NetID, but now cannot login.

  • Please do not take the word of the student that they finished the NetID Claim process.
  • Also, do not just send a password reset token, since this will not fix their issue. First, verify that the student completed their NetID Claim.


You can verify by going to Cerebro and looking at their Claim Eligibility.

  • If the student has Role student-claimed-<date> role and/or a Most Recent Claim Date or First Claim Date listed in the NetID Claim section, they have successfully completed their NetID Claim.
  • If there is a Claim Eligible Date (meaning the first day they could claim their NetID), Claim Eligible Status of Yes, and some value for midpoint Activation Code, they will need to go back and finish claiming. Instructions for claiming can be found here: NetID Claim, How to Claim for Students.

Still Needs to Claim!! Successfully Claimed


This can also be verified by going to the ISA tool and verifying if the Activation Token is set and if it is not, then they skipped finishing claiming and set their password via the NetID Center. If you don't see a password token in ISA check the AD lockout dashboard in Splunk. If it says they changed their password and it came from the iamfrontEnd that is the NetID Center (password page).

Please check in TDX for previous tickets to see if they received a password reset token/email. TeamDynamix, How do I Search and Filter Tickets in TDNext?

NetID Claim Error Messages

The following list is for consultants to determine which solution to use with each error message. Click to expand the appropriate section for the error message your customer is getting.

"The NetID Claim service is unavailable at this time. Please try again later." or "The NetID Claiming Service is not able to set your password at this time." 

  • This can mean they have not yet accepted admission, they have not waited 12 hours since accepting admission for the netid to be generated or they have an expired activation code. Check all three before continuing.
  • General error indicating that something is in need of repair, i.e. a file is missing, the database is down, Central Registry is unreachable, etc.
  • Have them try again later. If the issue persists or multiple people are reporting it, escalate the issue to HD Tier 2.

"We're sorry, but you are not eligible to claim your NetID at this time. Please contact the Tech Services Help Desk for further assistance."

  • The user's back-end data is somehow incorrect, and Help Desk intervention is required to fix the issue. 
  • Usual causes are that:
    • the UIN was found in some, but not all, back-end data stores (Central Registry, UIUC Active Directory, and midPoint)
    • the user has a uiucEduClaimEligible value that cannot be parsed as a date 
    • the user has neither an active type nor a valid claim date.(Claim Dates Expire after 1 year)
    • the user has reset their password in the Identity Center without claiming, which removes the activation code from their account and prevents them from claiming. This can happen if they were previously an affiliate or a student at another campus, if they do not finish claim and exit after they've set their recovery options and then change their password, or if someone sends them a one-time password reset token without checking to see if they've successfully finished claiming.
  • Check Cerebro to make sure there is a claim date (uiucEduClaimEligible), where the date is within the past year and an activation code is still set. If the date has expired (over a year in the past), transfer the ticket to TechSvcs-Identity and Access Management once we verify they are an admitted student (i-card, Cerebro, Banner, etc). If they have no activation code, they have reset their password and will need a new activation code created. Instructions for doing so can be found below:

    Only Tier 2 can generate new activation codes!
    1. First verify that the student has their old activation code (listed in Cerebro or the ISA tool).  The activation code should be displayed in the ISA Tool under the Activation Token (left hand side, 6 rows down).
    2. You should also have the student verify at least two other pieces of information (such as admit year or program) before proceeding.
    3. Once you have verified their information you can then click on the Generate Activation Token.
    4. This will generate a token you can see in the ISA Tool or Cerebro.
    5. The student will then be able to see their new activation in their acceptance letter (via MyIllini).

"An error occurred. Please wait a moment and try again."

  • General-purpose error when a back-end API request fails. This can sometimes come up when the service is under heavy load. (Hopefully simply trying the request again will work.)
  • This can also sometimes happen if midpoint or its servers are undergoing maintenance. Check the AITS - Service Desk - System Status Page - End User Guide to see if this is the case. (This often happens on Sundays)
  • NOTE: VERY RARE DO NOT ASSUME THIS IS THE ISSUE Finally, if the user has tried multiple times, it was not on a Sunday, and they keep failing at the same point, AND you have made sure they are entering everything correctly, are eligible, etc check the NetID History for the user in the ISA tool. If they have a previous deprovisioned account from another campus, that may be causing an issue and you should send the ticket over to AITS-IAM for troubleshooting. STATE THAT THIS IS WHY YOU HAVE ESCALATED IN THE TICKET. Just having a previous deprovisioned account is not sufficient evidence that this is the case; make sure you do your due diligence first!

"You are not eligible to claim your NetID using this application. Please contact your department for assistance."

  • The user is restricted from using NetID Claim, but does not have an active type. They are advised to seek help from their department.

"Unable to verify that UIN. Please try again later."

  • Unknown back-end error attempting to look up the UIN in Central Registry.
  • Escalate the issue to Tech Services.Identity and Access Management.

"That UIN does not exist. Please try again."

  • The user entered a UIN that was not found (or was found more than once) in Central Registry, Active Directory, and/or midPoint.
  • Try to find them in iCard based off of their name to see if they have two UINs. If they do have two UINs, transfer the ticket to HD Tier 2.
    • No NetID in iCard? Identity Management, No NetID exists in I-Card
  • It's possible that they are jumping the gun, and trying to claim before their admissions and enrollment are done processing.
    • This commonly happens with CITL students since there's another level of approval that needs to happen after they register for classes. See this KB: Help Desk, CITL Process for Non-Degree Student Registration.
    • Have a Tier 2 person check Banner for their admissions information.

Undergrads only: The Admissions checklist says they haven't claimed their NetID but they said they've already claimed.

  1. Check Cerebro and look at the claim section. If it says they are still claim eligible, they didn't finish claiming. For some reason there was an early exit before the last page. 
    1. If still claim eligible, have them return to http://netidclaim.illinois.edu/, they can input their UIN and same activation code they used before if they have not yet set up their recovery options. Their activation code should be in their MyIllini page. If they have set recovery options, they must use them to proceed.     
    2. Once they login, and they've already set a password, they will need to use 2FA to verify themselves and set a NEW password.    
    3. They will then be able to finish claiming, which automatically updates the checklist, and adds a student type and removes the inactive date in EDE. Cerebro will update as well that they have now claimed. Email will be automatically provisioned within 2 hours.
  2. If it says they already claimed, we'll need to confirm with TechSvcs-Identity and Access Management. Regardless, the checklist is only for following along, if they know their password and are active, they can proceed through the list.  

"No NetID exists for the specified valid UIN" - Or they don't have a claim date, are inactive and have the correct admit term

  1. If they say they are a new student, and they don't have a claim eligible date, ask HD Tier 2 to check Banner for SAAADMS.
  2. If they are a readmit/returning student, refer to NetID Claim, Deactivated, Returning, and Uni High students.
  3. If they do not see a NetID listed in i-card:
    1. Check to see if the UIN exists in Cerebro. i-card takes a day to update its NetID field after it's been created.
    2. If it doesn't exist in Cerebro either, have a HD Tier 2 check Banner (SAAADMS - check that there is a recent Entry Term and there is an Application Decision and Application Decision Date set) to see if they are admitted. 
    3. Make sure they have accepted admittance in myillini and have waited 12 hours for the NetID to be generated.
    4. Once confirmed, the ticket can be transferred to TechSvcs-Identity and Access Management to check why the netID hasn't been created. IAMU will create the netID and send the ticket back. 
    5. If there isn't confirmation they have been "officially admitted", they will need to contact either Undergrad or Grad Admissions depending on their student status - DO NOT give the application decisions from Banner to the customer.
Note: This happens a lot with graduate students. They are notified by their department that they've been admitted, but Graduate Admissions hasn't processed their information yet. Once that happens the student is notified again and then they can claim their NetID. If they are ahead of the paperwork and do not have an application decision date, HD Tier 2 can check that in SAAADMS. The Grad Student will have to wait for the notification that they can claim their netid.

"The NetID Claiming Service session has expired. Please login again to claim your NetID."

  • They sat idle for too long, they will need to start over at http://netidclaim.illinois.edu. If they still have issues, try another browser or clearing the cache and cookies.

"This NetID cannot be claimed at this time. The account has been locked since the number of allowed failed claiming login attempts has been exceeded. The lock will last for 10 minutes, after which you may try to claim your NetID."

  • They can try again in 10 minutes. We can also clear AD lockouts with the AD tool
