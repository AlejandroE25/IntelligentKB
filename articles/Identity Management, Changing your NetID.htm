Identity Management, Changing your NetID

Edit page
Can I change my NetID?

Help Desk Consultants, please escalate ALL NetID change tickets immediately to Tier 2.

We discourage changing a NetID due to the following:

  • You will lose access to your email for TWO BUSINESS DAYS while the change takes place.
  • It could take up to 2 weeks for all University systems to sync up with the new NetID
  • You may have trouble logging into some systems (course registration, Canvas, email) until the changes are fully completed
  • We have no control over services and systems outside of Technology Services.
  • We'll be unable to intervene in any systems that you're having trouble logging into until the change is fully completed
  • You will have to setup MFA again for the new ID.
  • You will have to sync your password again for the new ID by using the "change password" option in the NetID Center (NetID Center, How do I change my NetID Password?).

If you still wish to change your NetID after knowing the risks, in order to better meet the needs of our users, a one-time NetID change is permitted if one of the following reasons are met. Staff members can change their NetID once without having met these criteria:

  • You have had a legal name change (please have your name updated with the University before changing your NetID)
  • Your NetID contains an offensive word
  • Your NetID does not reflect your gender identity  
  • Your NetID has a misspelling or is otherwise confusing (e.g., has combinations of the number one (1) and the letter l)
    •   Help Desk consultants - feel free to ask Tier 2 or IAMU (via Teams) if you're unsure if this is applicable for someone.
  • Your NetID is hyphenated or in conflict with another campus user
  • Your NetID differs from your Enterprise ID or NetID at Chicago or Springfield (needs to be escalated to Identity and Access Management at Urbana) - please note that an Enterprise ID cannot be hyphenated

NOTE: If you use Exchange for your University email, you will lose access to your email for at least TWO BUSINESS DAYS while the change takes place.

FAQ:

How do I request a NetID change?

Please contact the Help Desk via consult@illinois.edu or 217-244-7000. It is highly preferred you come into the Help Desk to change your NetID.

What are the length and character requirements for a NetID?

See Identity Management, NetID creation character rules.

What happens to my Active Directory username when I change my NetID?

The Active Directory username will be updated to the new name within two business days. You should continue to use your original username until it fails to work, and then you should switch to the new username. Please note that your Active Directory password will remain the same after a NetID change.

Group membership is preserved after the rename.

What happens to my password and 2-factor authentication after the change?

During the change, you will need to sync your password with your new Enterprise ID by using the "change password" option in the NetID Center. When your old password stops working, please reset it at the NetID Center (go.illinois.edu/password).

In addition, once the change is complete you will need to re-enroll in MFA. This is also done in the NetID Center.

What happens to my Exchange username and email address when I change my NetID?

[Link for document 47973 is unavailable at this time] 

Emails sent to your old NetID email address will be forwarded to your new NetID email address for one year.

What happens to my Box account when I change my NetID?

U of I Box, NetID or name change 

I have not yet claimed my NetID, but I want to change it. Can I do so?

If you meet one of the criteria above for changing your NetID, you may change it before claiming. This process is actually smoother than changing the NetID after claiming.

Help Desk Internal:

Important Notes:

Faculty & Staff get 1 NetID change without meeting the requirements above.

A customer may change their NetID more than once ONLY if it is due to a legal name change and they present proof.

An additional acceptable reason for a NetID change (student or staff/faculty) would be if they changed their Preferred First Name to match their gender identity or if they have a different display name in Active Directory. 

Before changing a NetID:

First, enter the current NetID in the NetID Change tool and select "Check the status of a previous change request for this NetID" to ensure that the customer has not already had a previous NetID before proceeding. You can also verify this by looking at the uiucEduNetIDLinkage field in EDE.

Please verify their new name in Banner if possible before initiating the change.

Check the NetID Change Tool as it will present a list of 5 options that the customer may choose from. The requested NetID will contain parts of the customer's name, and for students, there is an additional requirement that it will contain a number.

Custom NetIDs

Help Desk Full Timers have the ability to change the NetID to a custom one that is not in the automatically generated list.

If the user wants to pick a specific NetID, they should send us their top 3 preferences. Make sure the options they give us follow the rules in Identity Management, NetID creation character rules.

  • Please note! The free-form text box in the NetID Change admin tool will allow and accept a NetID that is over 8 characters, so make sure that any NetIDs that you offer to the customer and input into the tool follow the character rules.

Once you have several potential NetIDs, escalate the ticket to Tier 2. They will verify that the desired NetID is available using the NetID Validate and/or NetID History sections in ISA and then change the NetID.

  • Tier 2: Make sure that when you search for the NetID, it says "The NetId _____ has not been used yet." If it is used in any other campuses, it is unavailable and we cannot use it for someone else.

Changing a NetID:

Once all of the above steps have been completed, you can visit the NetID change tool and enter their current NetID. Select "Change this NetID" and hit submit. 

Choose the desired NetID and click "Submit Change Request". Double-check EVERYTHING before clicking submit - the process is very difficult to reverse if you make a mistake.

Once the NetID change is submitted, note the Change ID number in the ticket, and also let the customer know in case they need to reference it if there are problems in the future.
