Installing and Activating Microsoft 365 (Office)

Microsoft 365, Office, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams, activation, install, NetID, license
[Internal Staff Note] Microsoft 365 licenses are tied to active enrollment/employment. Graduated students retain access for 30 days after graduation. Employees retain access while employed. For license conflicts, use the Microsoft 365 Admin Portal at admin.microsoft.com (admin account required).

Overview

Currently enrolled students and active employees are eligible for Microsoft 365 at no cost. This includes desktop applications (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams, OneNote) and 1 TB of OneDrive storage. You may install Microsoft 365 on up to 5 devices.

Downloading and Installing Microsoft 365

  1. Go to portal.office.com and sign in with your university email address (netid@university.edu).
  2. If prompted for a sign-in method, choose Work or school account.
  3. Complete Duo two-factor authentication if prompted.
  4. On the Office home page, click Install Office → Microsoft 365 apps.
  5. The installer will download. Run the downloaded file and follow the on-screen instructions.
  6. Installation typically takes 15–30 minutes depending on internet speed.

Activating Microsoft 365

  1. Open any Office application (e.g., Word) after installation.
  2. When prompted to sign in, enter your university email (netid@university.edu).
  3. You will be redirected to the university login page. Enter your NetID and password.
  4. Complete Duo two-factor authentication.
  5. Microsoft 365 is now activated and linked to your university account.

Microsoft Teams

Microsoft Teams is available as part of Microsoft 365 and is used for video conferencing, chat, and collaboration. Sign in with your university email address (netid@university.edu).

Troubleshooting

  • "You don't have a license" error: Ensure you are signing in with your university email (netid@university.edu), not a personal Microsoft account. If the error persists, contact the Help Desk to verify your license is assigned.
  • Installation fails or stalls: Disable any antivirus during installation. Ensure you have at least 4 GB of free disk space. Try downloading the offline installer from the Office portal.
  • Applications ask for activation repeatedly: Sign out of all Office apps, then sign back in with your university email. In Account settings, confirm the correct account is listed.
  • Can't find Teams in Office installation: Teams can be downloaded separately from teams.microsoft.com/download.
