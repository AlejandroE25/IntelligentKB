Resetting Your NetID Password

password, reset, NetID, account, locked, expired, forgot password, credentials
[Internal Staff Note] Agents can manually unlock accounts via the Identity Admin Portal at admin.netid.university.edu. Require identity verification (student ID or employee ID + date of birth) before performing any manual reset. Password resets propagate within 15 minutes; AD sync may take up to 30 minutes.

Overview

Your NetID password controls access to university systems including email, learning management, library resources, and campus Wi-Fi. If you have forgotten your password or your account is locked, follow the steps below.

Password Requirements

  • Minimum 12 characters
  • At least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special character
  • Cannot reuse the last 10 passwords
  • Expires every 365 days; you will receive email reminders 30, 14, and 7 days before expiry

Self-Service Password Reset

  1. Navigate to netid.university.edu.
  2. Click Forgot Password / Unlock Account.
  3. Enter your NetID (e.g., jsmith) and click Next.
  4. Complete identity verification using one of the registered methods:
    • Approve a Duo push notification
    • Answer your security questions
    • Use a backup email or phone verification code
  5. Enter and confirm your new password, then click Submit.
  6. Wait up to 15 minutes for the new password to propagate across all systems.

Changing Your Password When You Know the Current One

  1. Sign in to netid.university.edu with your current NetID and password.
  2. Click Change Password.
  3. Enter your current password, then enter and confirm the new password.
  4. Click Save Changes.

Account Lockout

Your account is locked after 10 consecutive failed login attempts within 30 minutes. The lockout automatically clears after 15 minutes, or you can unlock it immediately using the self-service tool at netid.university.edu.

Troubleshooting

  • If your new password is rejected, ensure it meets all requirements and does not duplicate a recent password.
  • If verification methods are unavailable (e.g., lost phone), contact the Help Desk in person with a photo ID.
  • If password changes are not taking effect, clear your browser cache or try a private/incognito window.

Contact

For additional assistance, contact the Help Desk at help@university.edu or call 555-0100.
